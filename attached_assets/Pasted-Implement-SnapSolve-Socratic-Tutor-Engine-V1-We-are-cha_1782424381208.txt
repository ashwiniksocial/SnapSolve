Implement **SnapSolve Socratic Tutor Engine V1**.

We are changing SnapSolve from an AI explanation system into an interactive AI tutor.

The tutor must behave like an excellent human teacher.

A human tutor never explains something once and assumes the student has understood.

Instead, they continuously check understanding, identify misconceptions, adapt their explanation, and only move on once the student demonstrates mastery.

This behaviour must now become the foundation of SnapSolve.

---

## Create a new teaching engine

Create:

```
src/services/socratic/

socraticEngine.ts

misconceptionEngine.ts

conceptQuestionEngine.ts

masteryAssessment.ts

guidedHintEngine.ts

dialogueEngine.ts
```

All engines must be modular and Firestore-ready.

---

## Replace passive explanations with guided teaching

After every explanation, the AI must immediately enter Tutor Mode.

The tutor should never ask:

"Did you understand?"

Instead, it must ask a carefully chosen conceptual question.

Example:

Instead of

"What is the answer?"

Ask

"Why did we choose this formula instead of another one?"

or

"What does 'at least one' actually mean?"

or

"If this value became larger, what would happen?"

Questions should test understanding, not memory.

## Misconception Engine

When a misconception is detected:

Do NOT simply reveal the answer.

Identify the exact misunderstanding.

Example:

Student confused favourable outcomes with total outcomes.

Student forgot complementary angles sum to 90°.

Student used the wrong sign.

Student confused velocity with speed.

Student forgot balancing rules.

The tutor must reteach only that concept.

Avoid repeating the entire explanation.

---

## Guided Hint Engine

Never reveal the answer immediately.

Hints should appear in levels.

Hint 1

Very small clue.

Hint 2

Direct the student's attention.

Hint 3

Show the next step.

Hint 4

Reveal the reasoning.

Hint 5

Reveal the full solution.

Track:

Hints requested

Highest hint level used

Time before requesting hint

Persistence before requesting hint

---

## Adaptive Questioning

Generate follow-up questions.

Difficulty depends on mastery.

Mastery below 40%

Very simple conceptual questions.

40-70%

Basic application.

70-90%

Mixed questions.

Above 90%

Challenge questions.

---

## Mastery Validation

Never mark a topic understood after reading.

Mastery must be demonstrated.

Require:

Three consecutive conceptual answers correct.

OR

Two conceptual answers plus one application question.

Only then update mastery.

Concepts requiring multiple sessions.

## Design Goal

The student should feel they are having a one-to-one conversation with an expert private tutor.

The tutor should ask more questions than it answers.

The tutor should guide rather than solve.

The tutor should never allow the student to become dependent on SnapSolve.

Every interaction should move the student toward solving future questions independently.

This feature becomes the central learning experience of SnapSolve.
