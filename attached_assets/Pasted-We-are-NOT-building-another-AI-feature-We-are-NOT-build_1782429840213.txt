===================================================================

1. LESSON PLANNER

Before writing,

AI must silently answer

What does the student already know?

What are the prerequisites?

Which ideas must be taught first?

Which ideas can wait?

What is the simplest teaching order?

Build the lesson around dependency order.

Never around textbook order.

===================================================================

2. CONFUSION PREDICTOR

For EVERY paragraph,

predict

"What question will the weakest student ask now?"

Generate every likely confusion.

Examples

What does numerator mean?

Why is denominator not zero?

Why can we square both sides?

Why is p even?

Where did this equation come from?

What is contradiction?

Immediately answer every confusion.

Never allow confusion to survive into the next paragraph.

===================================================================

3. MICRO TEACHING

Never explain more than ONE new idea at once.

Each paragraph should introduce only ONE concept.

After every concept,

pause.

Confirm understanding.

Then continue.

No paragraph should teach multiple unfamiliar ideas.

===================================================================

4. SCAFFOLDING

Teach exactly like a human teacher.

Move through these stages

Observe

↓

Recall previous knowledge

↓

Teach one idea

↓

Example

↓

Student thinks

↓

Next idea

↓

Example

↓

Student thinks

↓

Continue

Never jump ahead.

Never assume learning.

===================================================================

5. CHECKPOINTS

After every major concept,

ask a tiny question.

Examples

Which number is irrational?

Why did we square both sides?

What are we trying to prove?

These are not tests.

They are teaching checkpoints.

If the answer is wrong,

the lesson should automatically reteach the previous concept before continuing.

===================================================================

6. LESSON PACING

Estimate cognitive load continuously.

If too many new concepts appear together,

split them.

If a paragraph exceeds comfortable reading length,

split it.

Avoid overwhelming weak students.

The goal is understanding,

not information density.

===================================================================

7. ANALOGY ENGINE

Every abstract concept should be checked.

If it is difficult,

find a real-life analogy.

Only use analogies that genuinely improve understanding.

Never force them.

===================================================================

8. TEACHER DIALOGUE

Rewrite all explanations.

Every paragraph should sound like

"I am sitting beside you."

Avoid textbook language.

Avoid lecture style.

Use conversational teaching.

Example

Instead of

"Applying the distributive property..."

Say

"Let's open the brackets one step at a time. Think of it as giving every number inside the bracket its turn to be multiplied."

The student should feel guided,

not instructed.

===================================================================

9. LESSON QUALITY RULES

Every lesson must satisfy

One idea per paragraph

No unexplained terminology

No unexplained symbols

No unexplained formulas

No logical jumps

No skipped arithmetic

No skipped algebra

Every step has WHY

Every difficult concept has an analogy or intuition where helpful

Every major concept has a checkpoint

Every lesson ends with

"I can now..."

===================================================================

10. DO NOT ADD FEATURES

No new pages

No dashboards

No analytics

No widgets

No animations

No gamification

Improve only the teaching.

===================================================================

SUCCESS CRITERIA

A student who normally scores 20/100 should be able to

Read ONE lesson.

Understand every step.

Answer the follow-up question independently.

Explain the concept to another student in simple language.

Only then is the lesson considered successful.

This Master Teacher Engine becomes the brain that plans HOW to teach before the AI writes the lesson.
